The entire world has been mapped this way between zoom levels 2 and 10. So far, only the San Francisco Bay Area has been mapped to street level (zoom level 15). The plan is to add more cities as fast as time and life's usual bad craziness permit. We'll probably start with New York City but if there's some place you're keen to see mapped let us know.

This version (August 2010) of prettymaps is coded-named Isola after the Finnish textiles designer Maija Isola. At a time when the tools for making maps are becoming [WORDS] it is nice to look back at her work and imagine the maps she might have made if she were alive today.

It is also a fictional city in the writer Ed McBain's novels:

The town, Isola, in which these novels were set, was unmistakably New York. Since it did not declare itself as the Big Apple, it was not open to McBain to rely merely on a recital of the city's famous precincts, parks and landmarks to give his background authenticity. Isola lived, triumphantly in its own right, alongside New York but not simply mimicking it.

— The Times (of London)

Which is a healthy way to think about maps in general.
